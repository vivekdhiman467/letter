| Please Hear Me ❤

my apology

I'm sorry — truly, quietly, without excuse.

I know I made mistakes. Not small ones I can explain away, but ones that mattered — and I've stopped looking for reasons that make them smaller than they were.

I understand I hurt you. Not just the moment itself, but the quiet after it — the doubt it might have left behind, the trust it strained. I see that now, clearly, and it sits heavy with me.

I regret what happened more than I know how to say plainly. If I could take that moment and hand you something better instead, I would, without hesitation.

I never wanted to become the reason for your pain. You were never supposed to be hurt by the person who cares about you most — and knowing I became that, even briefly, is the part I sit with the longest.

I don't expect instant forgiveness, and I'm not asking for it here. Forgiveness, if it ever comes, has to be yours to give in your own time, on your own terms.

I'm truly sorry. Not the version of sorry that wants something in return — the version that just needed to be said honestly, once, without conditions.

I want to become a better person because of this, not despite it. And I know the only thing that will ever mean anything after words like these is what I do next.

My actions will prove my words. That's not a promise I'm asking you to wait for — it's simply the truth of what happens now.

my promise

Words are easy. So here is what I'll do instead.

Your feelings, respected

I will listen without defending, and let what you feel be true without needing to argue it away.

Honesty, even when it's hard

I will tell you the truth before it becomes something I have to explain later.

Thought before action

I will pause where I used to react, and choose my next step instead of stumbling into it.

Never intentionally again

I will never knowingly become the source of the pain I'm apologizing for right now.

Still learning, always

I will keep learning from this — not once, and not only when it's convenient to.

Trust, earned slowly

I will earn your trust back through consistency, not through asking for it.

about you

Some things I never want to say too quietly.

Your smile has a way of rearranging a room — quiet proof that good things are still possible.

Your eyes hold more honesty than most people manage in a lifetime of words.

Your kindness isn't performance. It's simply who you are when no one's asking you to be.

Your strength carried things I only saw the surface of, and still, you kept showing up gently.

Your intelligence isn't loud about itself, and that's exactly why it's the kind worth admiring.

Your laughter is the sound I'd choose out of every sound in the world, if I ever had to choose just one.

Your heart is generous in a world that doesn't always deserve that — mine included, on the day I forgot to.

a letter

My dearest,

I've started this letter more times than I can count, and thrown away every version that tried too hard to be forgiven. So here's the one that just tries to be honest.

I hurt you, and I've spent a long time sitting with what that actually means — not the version where I explain myself, but the version where I simply own it. I was wrong. I'm sorry. Not because I got caught in it, but because you didn't deserve it, not for a single moment.

You've been one of the gentlest, most patient people I've known, and somewhere I let that become something I took for granted instead of something I protected. That's on me, and I won't dress it up as anything else.

I'm not writing this to be let off easily. I'm writing it because you deserved to hear it plainly, in my own handwriting, without a screen's distance between us pretending to be closeness.

Whether you choose to forgive me or not, I will always respect your decision. I simply wanted you to know how deeply sorry I am.

With all the honesty I have,
Me
